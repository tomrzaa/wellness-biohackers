Frequently Asked Questions

What is hydrogen water?

Hydrogen water is regular water that has been infused with molecular hydrogen gas (H₂). Molecular hydrogen is a tasteless, odorless gas that dissolves in water. Research suggests hydrogen water may have antioxidant properties, though individual results may vary.

How do hydrogen water bottles work?

Most hydrogen water bottles use a process called electrolysis (SPE/PEM technology) to generate hydrogen. When activated, an electric current passes through the water using platinum-coated titanium plates, splitting water molecules and producing hydrogen gas that dissolves into the water. The process typically takes 3-10 minutes depending on the bottle and cycle selected.

What should I look for when comparing bottles?

Key factors include: independently verified hydrogen concentration (measured in ppm or ppb), bottle capacity, build quality and materials (look for borosilicate glass, BPA-free construction), platinum-coated titanium plates, PEM technology for by-product separation, battery life, ease of cleaning, warranty coverage, and overall user reviews. Third-party testing verification is crucial—avoid bottles with self-reported claims only.

Are ppm/ppb claims always reliable?

Not always. Many manufacturers self-report hydrogen concentration numbers without independent verification. Look for brands that provide third-party lab testing certificates from accredited facilities. The Hydrotech NZ bottle, for example, has been independently verified to reach up to 3800ppb (3.8ppm), while many cheaper alternatives only self-report their claims.
