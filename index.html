FAQ
Common questions
1) What does "ppm" mean for hydrogen water bottles? +
PPM is "parts per million" — a common way brands describe the concentration of dissolved hydrogen in water. Some brands also use ppb (parts per billion). Because testing methods and conditions vary, it's best to treat ppm as a comparison guide and verify via documentation where available.
2) Are stated output claims always comparable between brands? +
Not always. Output can change based on water quality, temperature, cycle time, and measurement method. When comparing bottles, look for transparent test context (water type, timing, method) and clear warranty/support terms.
3) How often should I run a cycle? +
Most users run a cycle per fill, then drink shortly after. If you're using hydrogen water for daily hydration, consistency tends to matter more than doing longer or more frequent cycles.
4) Does bottle material matter? +
Material impacts durability, taste, and cleaning. Glass can feel "cleaner" taste-wise for some people and is easier to inspect, while silicone components can help with impact resistance. Build quality and sealing matter just as much as material type.
5) Can I use tap water or do I need filtered water? +
Many people use tap water; filtered water can help taste and reduce mineral build-up over time. If your water is very hard, you may need more frequent cleaning/maintenance.
6) What should I prioritise when choosing a bottle? +
For most buyers: (1) clear specs and documentation, (2) warranty/support terms, (3) build quality and sealing, and (4) total value after NZD conversion and shipping.
